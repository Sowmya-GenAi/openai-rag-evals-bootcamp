# Performance Review Cycle

## Cadence

Acme Corp runs **two formal performance reviews per year**:

- **H1 review:** January self-assessments, manager assessments, and calibration; results delivered by mid-February.
- **H2 review:** July self-assessments, manager assessments, and calibration; results delivered by mid-August.

In addition, every employee should have **monthly 1:1s** with their manager focused on growth and career development. 1:1s are not a substitute for the formal review cycle.

## Inputs

A formal review combines four inputs:

1. **Self-assessment** — written by the employee.
2. **Manager assessment** — written by the direct manager.
3. **Peer feedback** — from 3–5 colleagues nominated jointly by the employee and manager.
4. **Cross-functional feedback** — for senior roles, additional input from cross-functional partners.

All written input is shared with the employee at the close of the cycle.

## Rating scale

Acme Corp uses a 5-point rating scale:

| Rating | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 1 — Below expectations | Improvement plan required. |
| 2 — Approaching | Inconsistent; targeted coaching. |
| 3 — Meets | Consistently delivers at level. **Default rating.** |
| 4 — Exceeds | Notably above level for the cycle. |
| 5 — Exceptional | Rare, calibrated; reserved for outstanding outcomes. |

Calibration is run at the team and org level to keep ratings consistent across managers. A manager **cannot unilaterally** assign a 5 — it must be calibrated.

## Compensation linkage

- **Annual compensation review** runs once per year, in March, using the most recent two reviews (H2 of last year + H1 of this year).
- Promotions can be proposed in either cycle; promotion decisions are made by the next-level-up manager and HR.

## Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)

A rating of 1 leads to a formal PIP. PIPs are **30, 60, or 90 days** depending on role and severity. Failure to meet PIP expectations may result in termination. PIPs are not punitive in intent — Acme Corp's stated goal is for the majority of PIPs to result in successful improvement.

## Disagreements

If you disagree with your rating, you may request a review by your manager's manager within **10 business days** of receiving the rating. Beyond that, the rating is final for the cycle but you may raise the issue in the next 1:1.
