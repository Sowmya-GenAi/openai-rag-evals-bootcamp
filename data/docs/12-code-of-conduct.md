# Code of Conduct

Every Acme Corp employee, contractor, and visitor is required to follow this Code of Conduct.

## Core principles

1. **Respect** — treat every colleague with dignity, regardless of role, background, or tenure.
2. **Honesty** — be truthful in your work, your reporting, and your interactions.
3. **Safety** — physical and psychological safety of others is non-negotiable.
4. **Stewardship** — Acme's data, equipment, and reputation are entrusted to you. Treat them accordingly.

## Harassment and discrimination

Acme Corp does not tolerate harassment or discrimination on the basis of race, color, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, age, disability, military or veteran status, or any other legally protected characteristic.

Harassment includes, but is not limited to: offensive jokes, slurs, intimidation, unwelcome physical contact, sexually suggestive comments, and the display or distribution of offensive materials. This applies in any work-related setting — office, remote, off-site events, customer events, social media, and chat platforms.

## Reporting harassment

You may report harassment through any of these channels:

- Direct manager, **if** the manager is not the subject.
- Skip-level manager.
- People Operations (`peopleops@acme.example`).
- Anonymous hotline (number and online form in the People Operations portal).

All reports are taken seriously and investigated. Retaliation against a person who reports in good faith is itself a serious violation and may result in termination.

## Conflicts of interest

Employees must disclose situations where personal interests could conflict with Acme's interests. Examples requiring disclosure:

- Outside employment, board seats, or paid advisory roles.
- Financial interest in a competitor, customer, or vendor.
- Romantic or familial relationship with another Acme employee in the same reporting chain.

Disclosures are filed in the **Compliance** portal. They are reviewed by the Ethics Committee within 10 business days. Most disclosures result in no action — the goal is transparency, not prohibition.

## Gifts and entertainment

You may accept gifts from customers, vendors, or partners up to a value of **$100** without disclosure. Above that, file a `gift-disclosure` ticket. Cash and cash-equivalents (gift cards) of any amount must be returned and disclosed.

You may not solicit gifts under any circumstance.

## Use of Acme name and resources

Do not represent your personal opinions as Acme's. Do not use Acme equipment, accounts, or time for outside business activity. Personal blogging and speaking are encouraged — clearly label them as personal.

## Consequences

Violations of this Code may result in coaching, formal warning, demotion, or termination, depending on severity. Serious violations (harassment, fraud, theft, intentional data exposure) typically result in immediate termination.
